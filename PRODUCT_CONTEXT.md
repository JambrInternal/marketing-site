# Product Context

## What this business is

An on-site AI agent for trades, field-service teams, and industrial operators.

It:
- Answers calls and texts instantly (24/7)
- Handles routine requests
- Escalates real emergencies
- Follows business-specific rules
- Reduces missed calls, interruptions, and after-hours stress

## What this business is NOT

- Not a generic AI platform
- Not a call center replacement pitch
- Not enterprise software jargon-heavy

## Primary audience

Primary buyer persona: The On-Call Owner / Ops Lead

Who they are:
- Hands-on operator keeping jobs moving and customers calm
- Close to the work, not buried in dashboards
- Carries the phone, literally or figuratively

Mindset:
- Proud of reliability and reputation
- Allergic to fluff and buzzwords
- Practical, skeptical, open to tools that save time or money
- Feels responsible when calls are missed or customers are unhappy

Primary buyers (ranked):
- Owner / Founder (most common in trades)
- General Manager
- Operations Manager / Service Manager
- Dispatch Supervisor / Office Manager (strong influencer)
- Customer Experience / Contact Centre Manager (utilities / industrial)

Utilities / industrial buyers:
- Manager of Customer Service
- Director of Operations
- Innovation / Continuous Improvement Lead (as sponsor)

## Core outcome

Never miss important calls without burning out the team.

## Industry focus

Core:
- HVAC, plumbing, electrical
- Mechanical contractors
- Field service and maintenance companies
- Construction service divisions

Expanded:
- Utilities (power, water, energy)
- Industrial operations (refinery, mills, plants)
- Municipal or regional service operators

## Company size sweet spot

- 5-50 employees (trades)
- Small-to-mid operational teams (utilities / industrial)

## Pain points (daily)

- Missed calls = lost jobs, especially after hours
- Too many quick calls interrupting real work
- After-hours coverage burns out owners or senior staff
- Dispatch / admin is the bottleneck
- Answering services are expensive and inconsistent
- Follow-ups slip (quotes, scheduling, callbacks)
- Hard to hire and retain good office staff
- Seasonal spikes overwhelm small teams
- Customers expect instant response, not voicemail

Emotional pain:
- Cannot fully disconnect from work
- Always on call
- Feels guilty when a customer is missed
- Frustrated paying humans for repetitive call handling

## Goals

- Never miss an important call
- Fewer interruptions during the day
- Peace of mind after hours
- Faster response times for customers
- Less dependence on fragile staffing
- A calmer office and happier crew
- Predictable, reliable operations
- Protect reputation without scaling stress

Unspoken desire:
- I want this handled without me thinking about it

## Buying triggers

- A missed emergency call causes a complaint or lost job
- Owner is woken up too many nights in a row
- Office staff is overwhelmed or quits
- Hiring a dispatcher feels expensive or impossible
- Seasonal surge (winter heating, storms, outages)
- Growth: more trucks, more jobs, same admin capacity
- Switching or complaining about an answering service
- Leadership mandate to improve customer response
- Utility outage or service incident exposes call volume limits

Moment of clarity:
- We cannot keep doing this the same way

## What makes them say yes

- Clear, immediate ROI (missed calls recovered, hours saved)
- Minimal setup and risk
- Does not replace people, supports them
- Works after hours first (low risk pilot)
- Speaks like their business, not a robot
- Founder-led, accountable relationship
